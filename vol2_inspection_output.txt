--- TOPIC v2_t10 (ADVANCED STEREO TECHNIQUES) ---
ID: v2_t10_b_1
Content: What does X/Y stereo technique use?
Explanation: Mid/Side allows you to control stereo width *after* recording. It requires a Cardioid Mid and Figure-8 Side mic.
Image: /images/svg/stereo_mid_side_diagram.svg
--------------------
ID: v2_t10_b_2
Content: What is the main advantage of X/Y stereo technique?
Explanation: Blumlein Pair uses two Figure-8 mics crossed at 90 degrees. It captures a realistic room image but is very sensitive to rear acoustics.
Image: /images/svg/stereo_blumlein_diagram.svg
--------------------
ID: v2_t10_b_3
Content: True or False: M/S stereo technique uses two cardioid microphones.
Explanation: ORTF mimics human hearing: Cardioid mics spaced 17cm apart at 110 degrees. It offers a good compromise between width and mono compatibility.
Image: /images/svg/stereo_ortf_diagram.svg
--------------------
ID: v2_t10_b_4
Content: What does M/S stand for?
Explanation: Always check stereo recordings in mono. If the sound disappears or gets thin, you have phase issues.
Image: /images/svg/phase_cancellation.svg
--------------------
ID: v2_t10_b_5
Content: What is a spaced pair (A/B) stereo technique?
Explanation: To hear M/S, you must decode it: Left = Mid + Side, Right = Mid - Side.
Image: /images/svg/stereo_mid_side_diagram.svg
--------------------
ID: v2_t10_b_6
Content: Which stereo technique gives the widest stereo image?
Explanation: XY and Blumlein are "Coincident" pairs, meaning the capsules are touching. This minimizes time-arrival differences (phase).
Image: /images/explanations/explanation_stereo_xy.png
--------------------
ID: v2_t10_b_7
Content: What is the ORTF stereo technique?
Explanation: Stereo width is defined by the *differences* between Left and Right. Identical signals are Mono.
Image: /images/svg/stereo_width_visualization.svg
--------------------
ID: v2_t10_b_8
Content: What does the Blumlein pair technique use?
Explanation: A Jecklin Disc is an acoustic baffle placed between spaced omnis to simulate the head's shadowing effect.
Image: /images/svg/jecklin_disc.svg
--------------------
ID: v2_t10_b_9
Content: What is the Decca Tree used for?
Explanation: Decca Tree: 3 Omni mics in a 'T' shape. Used high above conductors to record orchestras. Provides a massive, cohesive cinematic wall of sound.
Image: /images/svg/polar_pattern_omni.svg
--------------------
ID: v2_t10_b_10
Content: Which stereo technique has potential phase issues when played in mono?
Explanation: Binaural: Recording with a dummy head. It captures the shadowing effect of the human head and ears. only works on headphones.
Image: /images/svg/stereo_field.svg
--------------------

--- TOPIC v2_t11 (HOW MICROPHONES WORK) ---
ID: v2_t11_b_1
Content: How does a dynamic microphone generate electrical signal?
Explanation: All microphones are transducers: they convert acoustic energy (sound waves) into electrical energy (voltage).
Image: /images/svg/transducer_diagram.svg
--------------------
ID: v2_t11_b_2
Content: True or False: Dynamic microphones require phantom power to operate.
Explanation: Dynamic mics use a voice coil moving in a magnetic field. This sturdy construction makes them tough and able to handle high SPL.
Image: /images/svg/mic_dynamic_construction.svg
--------------------
ID: v2_t11_b_3
Content: How does a condenser microphone work?
Explanation: Condensers work like a capacitor across a battery (Phantom Power). The backplate is fixed, the diaphragm moves.
Image: /images/svg/mic_condenser_construction.svg
--------------------
ID: v2_t11_b_4
Content: What are the two plates in a condenser microphone called?
Explanation: Ribbon mics suspend a tiny metal strip in a magnet. They are fragile but have a very fast transient response.
Image: /images/svg/mic_ribbon_construction.svg
--------------------
ID: v2_t11_b_5
Content: Why are large diaphragm microphones considered "warmer" than small diaphragm?
Explanation: Phantom Power (+48V) travels up the XLR cable to charge the capacitor plates in condenser mics.
Image: /images/svg/phantom_power_switch.svg
--------------------
ID: v2_t11_b_6
Content: What physical law explains how dynamic and ribbon microphones work?
Explanation: Sensitivity measures how much voltage a mic produces for a given sound volume. Condensers are much more sensitive than dynamics.
Image: /images/svg/mic_sensitivity_graph.svg
--------------------
ID: v2_t11_b_7
Content: What responds to air particle velocity rather than pressure?
Explanation: Mic signals are tiny ("Mic Level"). A Preamp is required to boost them up to usable "Line Level".
Image: /images/svg/mic_preamp.svg
--------------------
ID: v2_t11_b_8
Content: Which microphone type has the fastest transient response?
Explanation: Professional mics use balanced XLR connections to reject electrical interference (hum/buzz) over long cable runs.
Image: /images/svg/balanced_cable.svg
--------------------
ID: v2_t11_b_9
Content: What is the natural polar pattern of a ribbon microphone?
Explanation: Cardioid pattern rejects sound from the rear, making it ideal for live use.
Image: /images/explanations/explanation_polar_patterns.png
--------------------
ID: v2_t11_b_10
Content: True or False: Condenser microphones have active electronics that require power.
Explanation: Condensers capture high-frequency detail and transients accurately.
Image: /images/svg/mic_condenser_construction.svg

ID: v2_t12_b_7

ID: v2_t12_b_8

Content: What does a boom stand allow you to do?

ID: v2_t12_b_10
