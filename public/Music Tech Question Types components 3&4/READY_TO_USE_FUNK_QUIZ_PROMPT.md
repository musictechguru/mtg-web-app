## GENRE-SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS FOR FUNK

**Production techniques to emphasize:**
1. **Analog synthesis for bass** - Minimoog Model D, ARP Odyssey, producing fat, punchy bass lines
2. **Slap bass recording** - Close miking, DI + mic blend, capturing percussive attack
3. **Clavinet processing** - Wah-wah, phasing, envelope filtering on Hohner D6
4. **Wah-wah and envelope filters** - Mu-Tron III, Cry Baby, on guitar and bass
5. **Horn section production** - Multi-mic techniques, tight, punchy sound, minimal reverb
6. **Talkbox effects** - Vocoder alternatives, Peter Frampton effect, synthesized speech
7. **Drum recording** - Close miking, dry sound, emphasis on attack and transients
8. **Analog tape production** - 16/24-track, tape saturation, natural compression
9. **Guitar effects** - Phasing (MXR Phase 90), flanging, clean funk tones
10. **Minimal reverb aesthetic** - Dry, direct sound, short room reverbs, tight grooves

**Historical context:**
Funk's peak era (1970-1985) coincided with:
- 16 and 24-track analog tape recording
- Early analog synthesizers (Minimoog, ARP)
- Classic guitar effects (wah-wah, phasing, envelope filters)
- Pre-MIDI production (played live and recorded)
- Analog mixing consoles (SSL, Neve)
- Limited outboard effects (plate reverb, tape delay)
- Emphasis on live performance and groove
- Direct, punchy sound aesthetic

**Typical FUNK instrumentation:**
- Synth bass (Minimoog, ARP)
- Electric bass (slap style)
- Clavinet / electric piano
- Drum kit (tight, dry tuning)
- Horn section (trumpet, trombone, saxophone)
- Electric guitar (clean, rhythmic)
- Talkbox
- Percussion (cowbell, congas, shaker)

**Effects commonly used in FUNK:**
- Wah-wah (Cry Baby, Mu-Tron)
- Envelope filter (Mu-Tron III)
- Phaser (MXR Phase 90, Maestro)
- Talkbox (Heil Talk Box)
- Analog delay (Echoplex, Roland Space Echo)
- Compression (1176, LA-2A on bass/drums)
- Short reverb (plate, room)

**Assessment opportunities in FUNK:**
- Clear instrumental separation and timbres
- Obvious effects (wah-wah is unmistakable)
- Synthesis techniques easily heard
- Comparison of analog vs digital production in remixes
- Historical production methods (tape-based)
- Evolution of synthesis and effects
- Genre-defining production aesthetic
