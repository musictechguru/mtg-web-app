## 7. Rock Production

### Rock Recording Techniques
**Classic approaches to recording rock instrumentation.**

**Drum Recording:**
- **Close Micing:** Individual mics on each drum
- **Overhead approach:** Stereo pair captures kit, close mics for detail
- **Room mics:** Capture ambience (1-10 feet from kit)
- **Parallel compression:** Heavy compression blended with natural drums
- **Typical chain:** Close mics + overheads + room mics (3 layers)

**Guitar Amp Micing:**
- **SM57 standard:** On-axis (bright) or off-axis (mellower)
- **1-2 inches from grill:** Standard placement
- **Ribbon mic:** Royer R-121 for smooth tone (blend with SM57)
- **Room mic:** Capture room ambience
- **Double-tracking:** Two takes panned left/right (width, thickness)

**Bass Recording:**
- **DI + Amp:** Blend clean DI with amp character
- **DI:** Direct clean signal (clarity, definition)
- **Amp:** Character, distortion, warmth
- **Typical blend:** 50/50 to 70% DI, 30% amp

**Vocals:**
- **Large diaphragm condenser:** Standard (U87, AT4050, etc.)
- **Dynamic for aggressive:** SM7B for rock/metal (controlled, tough)
- **Compression:** Multiple stages (1176 FET + LA-2A optical common)
- **De-essing:** Essential (sibilance from compression/EQ)

**Production Characteristics:**
- Natural, live feel
- Emphasis on performance
- Room ambience (not too dry)
- Dynamic (preserve punch)
- Raw energy captured

### Mixing Rock Music
**Techniques for powerful, punchy rock mixes.**

**Drum Mix:**
- **Kick:** Prominent, punchy (3-5kHz click, 60-80Hz thump)
- **Snare:** Loud, cracking (200Hz body, 3-5kHz snap)
- **Overheads:** Cymbals + kit glue
- **Room mics:** Width and ambience (compressed heavily)
- **Parallel compression:** Blend heavily compressed with natural (adds power)

**Guitar Mix:**
- **Panning:** Hard left/right for rhythm guitars
- **EQ:** Cut lows (80-120Hz), boost mids (avoid mud)
- **Width:** Double-tracking creates stereo width
- **Lead guitar:** Center or slight off-center, above rhythm

**Bass Mix:**
- **Center panned:** Mono low end (focus, power)
- **EQ:** Remove mud (200-400Hz), add presence (1-3kHz)
- **Compression:** Heavy (4:1-8:1), consistent level
- **Lock with kick:** Tight relationship (both prominent)

**Vocal Mix:**
- **Centered:** Lead vocal always center
- **Compression:** Obvious but musical (4:1-8:1)
- **Presence:** Boost 3-5kHz (cut through guitars)
- **Reverb:** Plate reverb typical (classic bright reverb)
- **Delay:** Slap-back or eighth-note delay (optional)

**Overall Mix Approach:**
- Loud, powerful, in-your-face
- Dense arrangement (many elements)
- Wide stereo field (guitars, backing vocals)
- Prominent vocals (fight for space)
- Dynamic (don't over-compress master)

**Famous Rock Producers/Engineers:**
- **Rick Rubin:** Minimalist, raw (Red Hot Chili Peppers, Metallica)
- **Butch Vig:** Polished rock (Nirvana "Nevermind", Smashing Pumpkins)
- **Bob Rock:** Big, powerful (Metallica "Black Album", MÃ¶tley CrÃ¼e)
- **Andy Wallace:** Clear, punchy (Nirvana "Nevermind" mix, Rage Against the Machine)

## 8. Electronic Music Production

### EDM/Dance Production
**Electronic dance music production techniques and workflow.**

**Sound Design:**
- **Synthesizers:** Primary sound source
  - Serum (wavetable) - modern standard
  - Massive (wavetable) - classic
  - Sylenth1 (subtractive) - leads, pads
  - FM8 (FM synthesis) - basses, bells
- **Layering:** Multiple synths per sound (thick, full)
- **Processing:** Heavy saturation, compression, limiting

**Kick Drum:**
- **Critical element:** Foundation of track
- **Layering:** Kick + sub (separate elements)
- **Sidechain:** Everything sidechains to kick (pumping effect)
- **Pitch:** Often tuned to key of track
- **Samples:** Vengeance, Splice libraries standard

**Bass/Sub-Bass:**
- **Mono:** Always mono (phase coherent)
- **Sidechained:** Ducks for kick (rhythm)
- **Saturation:** Distortion adds harmonics (audible on small speakers)
- **Lowpass filter:** Remove unnecessary highs (focus on lows)

**Drums:**
- **Samples:** Not live drums (programmed, samples)
- **Layering:** Multiple samples per sound (snare = 3-5 layers)
- **Transient shaping:** Tight, punchy
- **Quantized:** Perfect timing (no swing typically)

**Buildups:**
- **Rising elements:** Risers, white noise sweeps
- **Filters:** Opening low-pass filters
- **Reverb:** Increasing reverb into drop
- **Snare roll:** Accelerating snare hits (common)
- **Silence before drop:** 1 beat silence (tension)

**Drops:**
- **Maximum energy:** All elements, loud
- **Impact:** Strong kick + bass entrance
- **Call and response:** Vocal chops, stabs
- **Variation:** Every 4/8 bars (maintain interest)

**Arrangement:**
- **Intro:** 8-16 bars (minimal)
- **Buildup:** 8-16 bars (tension)
- **Drop 1:** 16-32 bars (energy release)
- **Breakdown:** 16-32 bars (calm, melodic)
- **Buildup 2:** 8-16 bars
- **Drop 2:** 16-32 bars (variation)
- **Outro:** 8-16 bars (fade or hard stop)

**Mixing EDM:**
- **Loud:** Very loud (heavy limiting)
- **Sidechain everything:** Pumping effect (characteristic)
- **Stereo width:** Very wide (except bass/kick - mono)
- **Bright:** Boosted highs (excitement)
- **Clean low end:** Only kick and bass below 100Hz

**Mastering EDM:**
- Target: -8 to -10 LUFS (louder than most genres)
- Heavy limiting (10-15dB gain reduction)
- Dynamic range: 4-6dB typical (very compressed)
- Bright, exciting, powerful

**Sub-Genres:**
- **House:** 4/4 beat, 120-130 BPM, groovy
- **Techno:** 4/4 beat, 120-150 BPM, minimal, hypnotic
- **Dubstep:** ~140 BPM (half-time feel), wobble bass, heavy
- **Trance:** 125-150 BPM, euphoric, melodic
- **Trap:** ~140 BPM, 808 bass, hi-hat rolls, rap influence
- **Future Bass:** ~140-170 BPM, lush chords, vocal chops

## 9. Hip-Hop Production

### Hip-Hop Beat Making
**Techniques for creating hip-hop instrumentals.**

**Drum Programming:**
- **Boom-bap (Classic):** Kick on 1 & 3, snare on 2 & 4
- **Trap:** Faster hi-hats (1/16, 1/32 notes), snare rolls
- **Swing/Shuffle:** MPC swing (laid-back groove, 55-65%)
- **Layering:** Multiple kicks, snares (thickness)
- **808 bass:** Pitched sub-bass (melodic, essential in trap)

**Sampling:**
- **Chop samples:** Slice loops into pieces, rearrange
- **Pitch shifting:** Change sample pitch (classic technique)
- **Time-stretching:** Change tempo without pitch
- **Filtering:** EQ, filtering for character
- **Flip:** Transform sample completely (creative)

**Melodic Elements:**
- **Loops:** Sample loops (vinyl, Splice, etc.)
- **MIDI:** Play melodies with plugins
- **One-shots:** Keys, guitars, strings layered
- **Minimalism:** Often sparse (space for vocals)

**Structure:**
- **Intro:** 4-8 bars (minimal or silence)
- **Verse:** 16 bars (minimal, space for vocals)
- **Hook/Chorus:** 8 bars (fuller, melodic)
- **Verse 2:** 16 bars
- **Hook:** 8 bars
- **Bridge/Outro:** 8-16 bars
- **Total:** ~3 minutes typical

**808 Bass:**
- **Pitched kick drum:** Creates bass line
- **Tuning:** In key with track
- **Decay:** Adjust for bounce (short = bouncy, long = smooth)
- **Distortion:** Add harmonics (hear on small speakers)
- **Mono:** Always mono (phase coherent)

**Vocal Production:**
- **Recording:** SM7B standard (tough, controlled)
- **Compression:** Heavy (6:1-10:1, obvious)
- **De-essing:** Essential (sibilance control)
- **Doubles/ad-libs:** Layer vocal takes
- **Autotune:** Effect or subtle correction (genre-dependent)
- **Delay:** Slap-back or tempo-synced

**Mixing Hip-Hop:**
- **Vocals loud:** Vocal centered, prominent
- **Kick + bass:** Foundation (powerful low end)
- **Minimal reverb:** Dry overall (vocals more upfront)
- **Stereo width:** Wider than vocals (but bass mono)
- **Loudness:** Competitive (-9 to -12 LUFS typical)

**Famous Hip-Hop Producers:**
- **Dr. Dre:** G-funk, clean, punchy (West Coast)
- **J Dilla:** MPC swing, sampling master (boom-bap)
- **Kanye West:** Soul samples, chipmunk vocals (creative)
- **Metro Boomin:** Modern trap, 808s, dark (Atlanta)
- **Timbaland:** Unique percussion, innovative (2000s)
- **Pharrell Williams:** Funk influence, minimalist
- **DJ Premier:** Boom-bap king, sampling, scratches

## 10. Pop Production

### Modern Pop Production
**Contemporary pop music production techniques.**

**Characteristics:**
- **Vocal-centric:** Vocals are star (everything supports)
- **Hook-focused:** Memorable, repeating hooks
- **Polished:** Very clean, produced sound
- **Wide stereo:** Impressive stereo width
- **Loud:** Competitive loudness (-9 to -11 LUFS)

**Vocal Production:**
- **Tuning:** Obvious Autotune or subtle (polished, perfect)
- **Compression:** Multiple stages (consistent, upfront)
  - FET (1176) for character
  - Optical (LA-2A) for smoothness
  - Final gentle compression (glue)
- **Reverb:** Lush but not drowning (plate, hall)
- **Delay:** Eighth-note or dotted-eighth (space, width)
- **Doubles:** Nearly always doubled (thickness)
- **Ad-libs:** Background vocals, harmonies (ear candy)

**Drums:**
- **Samples:** High-quality, processed samples (not live drums typically)
- **Layering:** Multiple samples per sound
- **Sidechain:** Kick sidechains bass (pump, space)
- **Reverb:** Snare has big reverb (impact)
- **Quantized:** Perfect timing (polished)

**Bass:**
- **Synth bass:** Often synthetic (not bass guitar)
- **Sub-bass:** Separate sub layer (low end)
- **Mono:** Low end always mono
- **Sidechain:** Ducks for kick

**Synths/Keys:**
- **Pads:** Lush, wide pads (fill space)
- **Arps:** Rhythmic arpeggios (movement)
- **Plucks:** Short, percussive synths
- **Layering:** Multiple synths (thick, full)

**Arrangement:**
- **Verse 1:** Minimal (build anticipation)
- **Pre-Chorus:** Build tension (add elements)
- **Chorus:** Maximum energy (all elements)
- **Verse 2:** Similar to Verse 1 (but slight variation)
- **Pre-Chorus 2**
- **Chorus 2:** Often bigger than Chorus 1
- **Bridge:** Different feel (contrast)
- **Final Chorus:** Biggest (all out)
- **Outro:** Fade or final hit

**Production Tricks:**
- **Vocal chops:** Sampled/sliced vocal phrases
- **Risers/Sweeps:** Build tension before drops
- **Impacts:** Big hits on downbeats
- **Reverse reverb:** Reversed reverb before vocal phrases
- **Stutters/Glitches:** Rhythmic repeats (ear candy)

**Mixing Pop:**
- **Vocals clear:** Nothing masks vocals
- **Wide stereo:** Pads, synths very wide
- **Mono bass/kick:** Focus and power
- **Bright:** Boosted highs (modern, exciting)
- **Loud:** Heavy limiting (competitive)

**Mastering Pop:**
- Target: -9 to -11 LUFS integrated
- Bright: High-shelf boost (+1-2dB at 10kHz)
- Wide: M/S enhancement (sides boosted)
- Loud: Heavy limiting (8-12dB GR)
- Clean: No distortion, polished

**Famous Pop Producers:**
- **Max Martin:** Mega-hits (Britney, Taylor Swift, The Weeknd)
- **Jack Antonoff:** Indie-pop (Taylor Swift, Lorde, Lana Del Rey)
- **Benny Blanco:** Modern pop (Ed Sheeran, Justin Bieber)
- **Ryan Tedder:** OneRepublic, BeyoncÃ©, Adele
- **Greg Kurstin:** Adele, Sia, Beck

## 11. Ambient/Experimental Production

### Ambient & Atmospheric Production
**Creating spacious, textural, immersive soundscapes.**

**Sound Sources:**
- **Field recordings:** Nature, urban, environments
- **Synthesizers:** Pads, drones (evolving textures)
- **Processed instruments:** Guitar, piano (heavily effected)
- **Granular synthesis:** Create evolving textures
- **Paulstretch:** Extreme time-stretching (drones from short sounds)

**Reverb:**
- **Essential:** Defines space (most important effect)
- **Long decays:** 3-10+ seconds typical
- **Layered reverbs:** Multiple reverbs (depth)
- **Convolution:** Realistic spaces (cathedrals, caves)
- **100% wet:** Often completely wet (no dry signal)

**Time-Based Effects:**
- **Delay:** Very long delays (500ms-2s+)
- **Feedback:** High feedback (80-100%)
- **Modulated delays:** Chorus/flanger-like modulation
- **Reverse reverb:** Ethereal, otherworldly

**Techniques:**
- **Layering:** Many subtle layers (depth, complexity)
- **Automation:** Slow parameter changes (evolving)
- **Randomization:** LFO, random modulation (organic)
- **Low-pass filtering:** Dark, mellow (reduce harshness)
- **Slow movements:** Nothing sudden (smooth transitions)

**Structure:**
- **No traditional structure:** Evolving, continuous
- **Long form:** 5-20+ minute pieces
- **Repetition with variation:** Subtle changes over time
- **No drums/rhythm:** Often (or very subtle)

**Mixing Ambient:**
- **Space:** Lots of reverb, delay (spacious)
- **Wide stereo:** Very wide (immersive)
- **Subtle:** Nothing jumps out (even, balanced)
- **Dark or bright:** Depends on mood (often darker)
- **Dynamic:** Preserve dynamics (don't over-compress)

**Mastering Ambient:**
- Target: -16 to -20 LUFS (quieter, more dynamic)
- Gentle compression (preserve dynamics)
- Long reverbs preserved (don't squash)
- Wide stereo maintained

**Famous Ambient Artists:**
- **Brian Eno:** Coined term "ambient music" (1978)
- **Aphex Twin:** Selected Ambient Works (electronic ambient)
- **Stars of the Lid:** Drone, orchestral ambient
- **Tim Hecker:** Noise, texture, experimental
- **Grouper:** Lo-fi, ethereal, vocals
