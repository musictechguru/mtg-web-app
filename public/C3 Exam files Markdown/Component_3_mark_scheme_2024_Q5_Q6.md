### AO4 - Impact of the Technology (max 15 marks)

#### 1940s/1950s/1960s/1970s - Tape Delay

- Analogue early on
- Mono early on
- Tape machines/tape loops/tape delay
- Space Echo/Copycat/Echorec/Echoplex/EchoSonic
- Popular in science fiction soundtracks
- Used by electroacoustic composers e.g. Stockhausen
- Creates swirling/resonant/layered/trippy sounds
- Lo-fi
- Delay time/rhythms controlled by tape speed/selecting heads
- Difficult to achieve accurate delay times/no tempo sync
- Reverse delays
- Tape speed change gives interesting pitch effects
- Overloading tape (with feedback)
- Regeneration/becomes more intense/lasts longer
- More than 100% feedback gives infinite/regenerating delays
- Saturation/soft clipping/pleasing distortion
- Restricted high end/warm sound
- Degrades over time so there are pitch/frequency response imperfections
- Erase head could be taken out (sound on sound)
- e.g. dub/reggae/punk/electronica/prog rock use tape delay
- Modulated effects/chorus/flange/phaser

#### Slap Back Delay

- e.g. rock and roll / Elvis
- Most widely used on vocals
- Slap back has a relatively short delay time/80-150ms
- Slap back has a single repeat/no feedback
- Slap back typically has a high send/wet level/loud repeat
- Slap back gives a doubling/layered effect without affecting the clarity of the music
- ADT also popular (in context of analogue delay)

#### Oil Can Delay/Drum Delay

- Late 1950s/1960s
- Oil can delay built into Gibson/Fender guitar amps
- More compact than tape delay
- Drum delay example is Binson/Echorec
- Drum delay used by Pink Floyd

#### Bucket Brigade/BBD Delay

- Late 1960s/1970s
- Solid state
- More compact than tape delay
- Guitar delay pedals in this era
- Used in karaoke machines
- Only can manage short delays/automatic double-tracking/ADT common
- Memory Man/Boss DM2
- Uses capacitors
- Easily overloads/limited dynamic range
- Saturation/soft clipping/pleasing distortion
- Restricted high end/warm sound
- Noisy/clock noise
- More than 100% feedback gives infinite/regenerating delays

#### Digital Delay/DDL

- 1970s/1980s
- Solid state
- More control over delay patterns/repeat volumes/multi-tap
- More accurate delay times / tempo/beat sync/tap tempo / more accurate copy of original signal
- Stereo/panned/ping-pong delay
- Stereo delay has different delay times/note values for each channel
- Stereo delay used for automatic double tracking/ADT
- Filtering/low pass/high pass
- Reverse delays
- Outboard unit/rack unit
- More than 100% feedback gives infinite/regenerating delays
- Guitar delay pedals in this era
- Used as a layering effect on guitars
- E.g. The Edge/U2
- MIDI control/automation
- Presets can be stored
- Digital delays are used to create reverb
- Modulated effects/chorus/flange/phaser
- Resonant/metallic delay on drums/vocals (in e.g. EDM)
- This uses very short delay times with feedback

#### Plug-ins/DAW

- 1990s/2000s
- Emulation/convolution of earlier types or digital delay
- More control of delay patterns/repeat volumes/multi-tap
- More accurate delay times
- Tempo/beat sync/tap tempo
- Stereo/panned/ping-pong delay
- Award stereo delay parameter setting e.g. different delay times/note values for each channel
- Stereo delay used for automatic double tracking/ADT
- Stereo delays give wider sound/more dimension/more space for other instruments in mix
- Filtering/low pass/high pass
- MIDI control/automation
- Presets can be stored
- More than 100% feedback gives infinite/regenerating delays
- Freeze/infinite sustain
- Delay is used to create reverb/modulated effects/chorus/flange/phaser
- Resonant/metallic delay on drums/vocals (in e.g. EDM)
- This uses very short delay times with feedback
